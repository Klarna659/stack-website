Vol. 01 — A Field Note on Tracking

The quiet art of tracking what you take.

By Stack Issue 01 · 2026 6 min read

Supplements, peptides, medications, GLP-1, vitamins — somewhere along the way, the routine became a spreadsheet. Stack is a quieter way to keep it all in one place.

Most of us start with one vitamin. Then a second. Then a stack. The routine grows, the doses shift, the reminders pile up in three apps that don't talk to each other. Stack is built for the way that actually happens — one place to log a dose, see what's left, and watch the trend.

The app does the math you'd otherwise do in your head. Reconstitution. Half-life. Refill timing. Every compound carries its own color, schedule, and supply. Open it in the morning, log a dose in a tap, close it. That's it.

There are no accounts, no analytics, no ads. Your data lives on your device, encrypted. You can export a backup whenever you want. You can wipe it all in two taps. This is not a feature list — it's a design choice. The point of a personal tracker is that it stays personal.

Stack works for supplements and vitamins. It works for GLP-1 and TRT. It works for peptides and protocols. The name comes from how people actually talk about what they take — your stack. The app meets that language.
