Stack is a private, on-device ledger for everything you put in your body — and nothing more. No accounts. No advice. No diagnoses. Just the quiet act of writing it down, every day, in seconds, on a screen that lives in your pocket.

The app was built for the new generation of people running real protocols at home: testosterone replacement, GLP-1 weight management, peptides, nootropics, daily vitamins, prescribed medications. Most of them are using a notes app, a spreadsheet, or nothing at all.

What it does

You add a compound, and Stack handles the rest — schedule, half-life, refill projection, body-map for injection rotation, an encrypted record you can export at any time. Scan a label and the app pre-fills the math.

What it doesn't

Stack will not tell you what to take. It will not flag a dose as "good" or "bad." It will not sell your data, because there is none to sell — every byte lives on your device, encrypted with a key only you hold.

The streak that forgives

The novelty: a streak system that grants one grace day per perfect week. The goal is adherence over time, not punishment for a single missed dose. The streak should encourage you, not shame you.
